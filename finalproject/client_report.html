Client / Developer Team Report

A reflection on my experience in each role during the WDD 130 team project.

Client Report

As the client, I was responsible for creating the site plan for my personal website and communicating my vision to my development team.

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I started by thinking about what I wanted the site to be. I decided to keep the content around my own personal hobby, focusing on my passion for fly fishing.

How did you communicate with your developer? Were there any issues with communication?

I communicated with my lead developer primarily through class time and text messages. I shared my completed site plan early so they had a clear reference for colors, fonts, and layout expectations. Overall communication was smooth and I felt like stewart and riley genuinely tried to follow the plan.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, for the most part. The home page came out close to what I had envisioned from the wireframe. The nav, color scheme, and overall structure matched the site plan well. There were some minor differences in spacing and font sizing, but nothing that broke the design. I appreciated that Stewart asked questions rather than just guessing when something was unclear.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I would put more detail into the wireframe upfront. I kept mine fairly simple, and a few times stewart had to make judgment calls on layout that I then wanted changed. More detailed wireframes for each section so I could see more progress before the final submission.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The most challenging part was time management with all of my other classes. I am currently doing this on extended time, so no I was not.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I liked seeing my site come to fruition. It was nice not to have to build my own idea

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would fix the communication issue, and make sure people are incentivized to actually come to class

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

I think so. It gave me a realistic feel for what communicating in corporate is like, and to not expect people to respond in a timely manner all the time
